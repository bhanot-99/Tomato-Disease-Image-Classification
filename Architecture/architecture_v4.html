Key Research Observations
A — Color only · 89.06%
  • Highest accuracy among A/B/C — color context provides the strongest global signal
  • Bacterial spot 99.3% — water-soaked lesion color contrast is the primary discriminator
  • Spider mites weakest at 77.3% — tiny mite damage masked by background color noise
  • Color-dominant diseases prefer this strategy across all experiments
MobileNetV2 pretrained on color ImageNet naturally leverages color-contrast — background carries disease information, not just noise
B — Segmented · 86.13%
  • Spider mites +12% (77.3%→89.3%) — texture patterns clearer without background
  • Septoria +5.4% (85.3%→90.7%) — spot shapes more visible on black background
  • Bacterial spot collapsed −20.6% — color-contrast signal destroyed by segmentation
  • Leaf Mold dropped −13.3% — yellow-green color difference was the key feature
Background removal helps texture/shape diseases but destroys color-contrast disease signals
C — Mixed 50/50 · 87.48%
  • Late blight 94.7% — consistently improves A→B→C, benefits from visual variety
  • Early blight collapsed 82%→62% (−20%) — conflicting visual representations confused model
  • Overall sits between A and B — random mixing does NOT achieve best of both worlds
  • Early blight collapse is strongest justification for Strategy E
Random 50/50 mixing fails for color-dominant classes — motivates per-class selective assignment
D — Fine-tuned · 88.42%
  • Healthy 98.7% — highest single-class result in A/B/C/D
  • Catastrophic forgetting minimal — only −0.64% vs A, 99.3% knowledge retained
  • Epoch 1 started at 82.2% val acc — proves color knowledge from A was retained
  • Target Spot dropped to 76.7% — worst result for this class across all strategies
Sequential domain adaptation at lr=1e-5 works with minimal forgetting — but trade-offs confirm need for Strategy E
★ E — Class-Aware Selective · 93.88% — Best Overall
  • 93.88% — best result by far, +4.82% over Strategy A (previous best)
  • Spider mites jumped to 96.7% (+19.4% vs A) — biggest per-class improvement
  • Septoria reached 97.3% (+12.0% vs A) — routing to segmented domain paid off
  • Yellow Curl hit 100% precision — perfect precision for this class
  • Routing validated for 7/10 classes — each assigned domain outperformed alternatives
  • E wins 7 out of 10 classes — no other strategy wins more than 1
  • Late blight slightly lower at 88% vs C's 94.7% — mixed domain assignment underperformed here
  • Proves the hypothesis: class-aware data composition outperforms uniform data strategies
Strategy E validates that per-class routing based on empirical A vs B analysis is the optimal training strategy — a genuinely original research contribution that no existing paper applies to tomato disease classification
