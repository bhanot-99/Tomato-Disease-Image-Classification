Key Research Observations — All 4 Strategies
Strategy A — Color only · 89.06%
  • Highest overall accuracy — color context is the strongest signal overall
  • Bacterial spot near-perfect at 99.3% — relies on color contrast of water-soaked lesions against green leaf
  • Healthy leaf detection at 94% — vibrant green color is a strong healthy indicator
  • Spider mites weakest at 77.3% — tiny mite damage patterns are obscured by background color noise
  • Color-dominant diseases (Bacterial spot, Leaf Mold, Early blight) consistently prefer this strategy across all experiments
Finding: MobileNetV2 pretrained on color ImageNet leverages color-contrast signals naturally — background context carries disease information, not just noise
Strategy B — Segmented only · 86.13%
  • Spider mites jumped from 77.3% → 89.3% (+12%) — texture patterns clearer without background distractions
  • Septoria improved 85.3% → 90.7% (+5.4%) — spot shape and pattern more visible on clean leaf
  • Yellow Leaf Curl highest at 96.7% — curl shape is a pure structural feature, unaffected by background removal
  • Bacterial spot collapsed from 99.3% → 78.7% (−20.6%) — color-contrast disease loses its primary signal
  • Leaf Mold dropped 92.3% → 79.0% (−13.3%) — yellow-green color difference was the key discriminator
  • Texture/shape-dominant diseases benefit from segmentation; color-contrast diseases are hurt
Finding: Background removal is a double-edged sword — it helps pattern-based diseases but destroys color-based disease signals
Strategy C — Mixed 50/50 · 87.48%
  • Late blight best result at 94.7% — consistently improves across all strategies (A→B→C trend)
  • Mosaic virus improved to 93.8% — combining both modalities recovers from the segmented drop
  • Target Spot best at 87.3% across A, B, C — responds well to visual variety in training
  • Early blight collapsed from 82.0% → 62.0% (−20%) — most critical failure, conflicting visual representations confused the model
  • Overall accuracy (87.48%) between A and B — random mixing does not achieve the best of both worlds
  • Random 50/50 mixing directly motivates Strategy E — per-class assignment needed, not uniform mixing
Finding: Early blight's 20% collapse under random mixing is the strongest evidence that class-aware selective mixing (Strategy E) is necessary
Strategy D — Fine-tuned · 88.42%
  • Healthy leaf reached 98.7% — highest single-class result across all 4 strategies
  • Mosaic virus reached 96.4% — best result for this class, D uniquely excels here
  • Catastrophic forgetting: only −0.64% vs Strategy A — 99.3% of original knowledge retained
  • Epoch 1 started at 82.2% validation accuracy — proves color knowledge carried over from A
  • Target Spot dropped to 76.7% — worst result for this class across all strategies
  • Bacterial spot dropped from 99.3% → 88.7% (−10.6%) — some color-contrast forgetting occurred
  • Fine-tuning is successful for sequential domain adaptation — overall performance competitive at 88.42%
Finding: Fine-tuning at lr=1e-5 successfully adapts to segmented domain with minimal forgetting, but per-class trade-offs remain — validating the need for Strategy E
