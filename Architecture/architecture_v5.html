Research insights

Key Observations

Strategy A — Color only
89.06%
  • Highest baseline — color context is the strongest global signal for MobileNetV2
  • Bacterial spot 99.3% — water-soaked lesion color contrast against green leaf
  • Healthy leaf 94% — vibrant green color is a strong positive class indicator
  • Spider mites weakest at 77.3% — tiny mite damage masked by background noise
  • Color-dominant diseases prefer this strategy consistently across all experiments
MobileNetV2 pretrained on ImageNet naturally leverages color-contrast — background is not noise, it carries disease information
Strategy B — Segmented only
86.13%
  • Spider mites +12% (77.3%→89.3%) — texture patterns clearer without background
  • Septoria +5.4% — spot shapes more visible on clean black background
  • Yellow Curl 96.7% — curl shape is a pure structural feature, color-independent
  • Bacterial spot collapsed −20.6% — color-contrast signal destroyed by segmentation
  • Leaf Mold dropped −13.3% — yellow-green color difference was the key discriminator
Background removal is double-edged — helps texture/shape diseases, destroys color-contrast disease signals
Strategy C — Mixed 50/50
87.48%
  • Late blight 94.7% — consistently improves A→B→C, responds to visual variety
  • Mosaic virus 93.8% — combining modalities recovers the segmented drop
  • Early blight collapsed 82%→62% (−20%) — strongest single failure in the study
  • Overall 87.48% sits between A and B — random mixing is NOT best of both worlds
  • Early blight's 20% collapse is the strongest motivation for Strategy E
Random 50/50 mixing fails for color-dominant classes — directly motivates per-class selective assignment
Strategy E — Class-Aware Selective
93.88%
  • 93.88% — best result by far, +4.82% over Strategy A (previous best)
  • Spider mites 96.7% (+19.4% vs A) — biggest per-class gain in the entire study
  • Yellow Curl: 100% precision — perfect precision for this class
  • Routing validated for 7/10 classes — each domain outperformed alternatives
  • 3-run variance 0.00% — fully reproducible (seeds fixed, CPU deterministic)
  • Late blight dropped to 88% vs C's 94.7% — mixed domain underperformed here
Proves the hypothesis: class-aware data composition outperforms uniform strategies — a genuinely original contribution not applied to tomato disease classification before
