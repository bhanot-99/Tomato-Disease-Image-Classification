Grad-CAM Visualization
Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping
What it does: Grad-CAM opens the model's black box by computing gradients of the predicted class score with respect to the final convolutional feature maps. These gradients are globally averaged to produce channel-wise importance weights, then linearly combined with the feature maps to generate a spatial heatmap — red regions indicate pixels that most strongly influenced the prediction.

Why it matters: Running Grad-CAM on all 4 trained models on the same leaf image produces 4 heatmaps side by side. This visually proves why color-contrast diseases drop under segmentation (the model loses its color focus area) and why texture-dominant diseases improve (attention tightens onto lesion patterns without background scatter).

Research value: Transforms numerical accuracy results into visual proof — a reviewer can see the mechanism, not just the numbers.

Disease Severity Estimation
Pixel-ratio analysis on segmented images
What it does: Using the segmented images (black background, leaf only), the system isolates the leaf area by counting non-black pixels, then detects diseased pixels by applying HSV color thresholding tuned per disease type. The ratio of diseased pixels to total leaf pixels yields a continuous severity score without any manual labeling.

Formula: severity = diseased_pixels / total_leaf_pixels

Why it matters: PlantVillage has no severity labels — this method creates a new data dimension from existing images. A farmer's system that says "Early Blight — Moderate (28% leaf affected)" is far more actionable than one that just says "Early Blight." This is an original research contribution — no existing benchmark defines severity this way for tomato diseases.

Key Research Observations — All 4 Strategies
Strategy A — Color only · 89.06%
  • Highest overall accuracy — color context is the strongest signal for MobileNetV2
  • Bacterial spot near-perfect at 99.3% — water-soaked lesion color contrast is primary discriminator
  • Healthy leaf at 94% — vibrant green color is a strong healthy indicator
  • Spider mites weakest at 77.3% — tiny damage patterns obscured by background color noise
  • Color-dominant diseases consistently prefer this strategy across all experiments
MobileNetV2 pretrained on color ImageNet naturally leverages color-contrast signals — background context carries disease information, not just noise
Strategy B — Segmented only · 86.13%
  • Spider mites jumped from 77.3% → 89.3% (+12%) — texture patterns clearer without background
  • Septoria improved 85.3% → 90.7% (+5.4%) — lesion shape more visible on clean leaf
  • Yellow Curl highest at 96.7% — curl shape is a pure structural feature
  • Bacterial spot collapsed from 99.3% → 78.7% (−20.6%) — loses color-contrast signal
  • Leaf Mold dropped 92.3% → 79.0% (−13.3%) — yellow-green difference was the key discriminator
Background removal is a double-edged sword — helps pattern-based diseases but destroys color-based disease signals
Strategy C — Mixed 50/50 · 87.48%
  • Late blight best result at 94.7% — improves consistently across A→B→C
  • Mosaic virus recovered to 93.8% — combining modalities rescues the segmented drop
  • Target Spot best at 87.3% — responds well to visual variety in training
  • Early blight collapsed from 82.0% → 62.0% (−20%) — conflicting visual representations confused model
  • Random 50/50 does not achieve best of both worlds — directly motivates Strategy E
Early blight's 20% collapse is the strongest evidence that class-aware selective mixing (Strategy E) is necessary, not uniform mixing
Strategy D — Fine-tuned · 88.42%
  • Healthy leaf reached 98.7% — highest single-class result across all 4 strategies
  • Mosaic virus at 96.4% — D uniquely excels, benefits from cross-domain exposure
  • Catastrophic forgetting: only −0.64% vs A — 99.3% of original knowledge retained
  • Epoch 1 started at 82.2% val accuracy — proves color knowledge carried over
  • Target Spot dropped to 76.7% — worst result for this class across all strategies
  • Bacterial spot dropped 99.3% → 88.7% (−10.6%) — minor color forgetting occurred
Fine-tuning at lr=1e-5 successfully adapts to segmented domain with minimal forgetting — per-class trade-offs remain, validating Strategy E
